## Semantic Roles

- Black:
  - structural grounding
  - dark sections
  - footer
  - strong contrast UI
- White / Warm White:
  - primary reading surfaces
  - cards
  - light sections
- Gold:
  - brand warmth
  - eyebrow text
  - headline highlight word
  - badges and subtle emphasis
- Green:
  - primary CTA
  - “start here” energy
  - trust/action color
- Red:
  - selective emphasis
  - conviction moments
  - occasional special CTA only

## Button Hierarchy

### Primary CTA

- Background: Brand Green
- Text: White
- Hover: Brand Green Deep
- Use for primary progression actions

### Secondary CTA

- On light backgrounds:
  - black fill or white with dark border, depending on context
- On dark backgrounds:
  - translucent white surface with white text
- Use for secondary navigation or lower-priority actions

### Accent / Special CTA

- Background: Brand Red
- Text: White
- Hover: Brand Red Deep
- Use sparingly

### Gold Usage

Gold is not the default button fill.

Use gold mainly for:

- eyebrow labels
- highlight words
- badges
- small emphasis moments

## Typography Color Rules

- Main headings:
  - black on light backgrounds
  - white on dark backgrounds
- Highlight one word or one segment in gold
- Body text:
  - `#111111` or `#5F5F5F` on light surfaces
  - softened white on dark sections
- Avoid multi-accent headline competition
- Let typography carry the premium feeling before color does

## Heading Accent Rules

Do:

- use one gold highlight word in a hero or section title
- use gold or green for small eyebrow text depending on meaning
- keep the rest of the heading in black or white

Don’t:

- color every word
- mix gold, green, and red loudly in one short headline
- use gold for long body copy on white surfaces

## Section Rhythm Guidance

The site should alternate intentionally between:

- dark grounded sections using Brand Black / Soft Black
- warm light reading sections using Warm White / White
- gold accents for warmth and identity
- green for primary action
- red only for selective punch

## Shadcn / UI Primitive Guidance

- Buttons:
  - rounded, assertive, uppercase, disciplined
- Badges:
  - gold by default, never generic blue-pill styling
- Cards:
  - warm white or white with soft borders and rounded corners
- Inputs:
  - rounded and calm, not harshly utilitarian
- Separators:
  - subtle, warm, never heavy gray by default

## Do / Don’t

Do:

- keep black and white as the structural base
- keep gold as the main visual signature
- use green as the default action color
- use red with restraint
- maintain contrast and readability

Don’t:

- turn every section gold
- treat green and red as equal-volume accents everywhere
- default to generic startup blue/gray styling
- overdecorate headlines when strong type already does the job
